Moscow, Russia

My first time living abroad was during my study abroad semester in Moscow, Russia. Since I studies Russian in undergrad, it was essential that I travel to this mysterious country in order to get a feel for the culture and customs of the people there. I studied in Moscow State Humanities University and took many wonderful classes which helped me improve my Russian speaking abilities.

Voronezh, Russia

After graduating from Truman State University in Kirksville, MO, I was awarded a Fulbright grant which allowed me to live and work in a different Russian city for a full academic year. I was assigned to Voronezh, a mid-size city south of Moscow, near the border with Ukraine. Here I taught English in Voronezh State University and made several friends from all over Europe.

Tartu, Estonia

At the end of my time in Russia, my friend Heidi convinced me to apply for a Master's Degree program at the University of Tartu in Estonia. If you've never heard about Estonia, I'm not surprised. I didn't know much about it myself before moving there. Estonia is a tiny country in Northeastern Europe, west of Russia, north of Latvia, south of Finland. With a population of only 1.3 million, it is quite small and thus easy to get around and explore. I was accepted into the Semiotics program at the University, so I packed up my things and moved next door to Estonia to begin my two-year-long studies.

St. Louis, MO

After finishing the Master's program in Estonia, with my shiny new degree in hand, I decided to move back home to St. Louis. After three years abroad, I was feeling a bit homesick. I had missed holidays and special events and had scarcely seen my family and friends, so I knew it was time to come home. Since returning home I have been searching for the next step in all sorts of different directions. Will Launchcode be the stepping stone that leads to the next path? Only time will tell.
